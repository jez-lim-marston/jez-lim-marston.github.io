Research Projects

A curated list of work in viral genomics, endogenous retroviruses, and single-cell computational biology, with related and ongoing interests in immunology and biosecurity.

Primary Projects

scTEQuant: viral & transposable-element quantification

Bayesian quantification · single-cell & bulk RNA-seq

Postdoctoral research, Cesarman Lab, Weill Cornell Medicine

A two-stage Bayesian pipeline for quantifying viral and transposable-element transcription from single-cell and bulk RNA-seq, developed with AI-assisted tooling and benchmarked head-to-head against established methods.

  • Extends genome-wide coverage roughly 280-fold beyond the ceiling where existing methods become computationally intractable
  • Improves quantification accuracy for repetitive and virus-derived loci
  • Built for direct application to EBV and HIV-1 transcriptional profiling
Key paper: Reyes-Gopar H*, Marston JL*, et al. (2025). A single-cell transposable element atlas of human cell identity. Cell Reports Methods. *Co-first authors

Single-cell viral programs in EBV+ and EBV/HIV lymphoma

Viral latency & reactivation · tumor immunology

Postdoctoral research, Cesarman Lab, Weill Cornell Medicine

Resolving EBV lytic-versus-latency programs and HIV-1 transcriptional reservoirs at single-cell resolution across a cohort of EBV+ lymphoma patients, including EBV/HIV co-infected cases.

  • Maps viral reactivation states onto the tumor and microenvironment transcriptional landscape
  • Characterizes consequences of co-infection in immunocompromised hosts
  • Applies scTEQuant to jointly quantify host, EBV, and HIV-1 transcription

A single-cell atlas of endogenous retrovirus expression

HERVs · locus-specific quantification

Doctoral research, Nixon Lab

Built the first locus-specific single-cell atlas of transposable element and endogenous retrovirus expression across the human immune system, using the Stellarscope pipeline.

  • Resolves individual HERV loci rather than family-level aggregates
  • Links HERV expression patterns to specific immune cell identities and states
  • Foundation for downstream work on HERVs in lymphoma subtypes and autoimmune disease

Related & Ongoing Interests

These areas intersect naturally with my core work on viral latency and endogenous retroviruses. I follow the literature closely, contribute where I can, and expect to develop them into full projects over time.

Antiviral immunology

Innate & adaptive control of viral reservoirs

How innate sensing and adaptive immune pressure shape EBV and HIV-1 reservoirs, and how single-cell transcriptomics can resolve the immune states that permit or suppress viral reactivation.

Skin immunology

Barrier immunity & cutaneous viral latency

The skin as a site of immune surveillance and viral latency for herpesviruses, and how resident immune populations at barrier tissues differ from the systemic compartments I study in blood and lymphoid tissue.

Autoimmunity

HERV expression & molecular mimicry

Associations between endogenous retrovirus expression and autoimmune disease, including molecular mimicry between HERV-encoded antigens and self-antigens as a possible driver of autoreactivity.

Computational biology

Methods development & AI-assisted tooling

Broader interest in computational methods for single-cell and genomic data, and in using AI tools to accelerate development and benchmarking of new quantification approaches.
